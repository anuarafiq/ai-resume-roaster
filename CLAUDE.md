# AI Resume Roaster

## Design Context

### Users
Students and early-career job seekers submitting their first (or fifth nervous) resume. They're not HR professionals - they want blunt, specific feedback without feeling humiliated. Used on a whim, any time of day, often right before hitting "apply."

### Brand Personality
Encouraging, wry, unpolished-in-a-good-way. Like a sharp friend who reads your resume and tells you the truth, but still wants you to get the job. The "roast" is the hook; the feedback underneath has to be credible enough to act on.

### Aesthetic Direction
Dark theme - moodier, more focused, treats the resume like it's under a spotlight. Playfulness lives in color, motion, and microcopy, not in goofy illustrations or bouncy UI. Anti-reference: generic AI-tool landing pages (centered hero, gradient text, purple-to-blue glow, Inter everywhere).

Typography: **Bricolage Grotesque** for display (hero, headings) - a grotesque with just enough irregularity to feel human, not corporate. **Public Sans** for body/UI text - plain, legible, civic-honest, the credible counterweight to the display font's personality.

Color: warm coral/ember accent (leans into "roast" literally - heat, not neon) on a dark, faintly warm-tinted charcoal surface. No purple-blue gradients, no neon cyan glow.

### Design Principles
1. Approachable warmth over corporate polish - the reader should feel coached, not judged.
2. Confident restraint - playfulness lives in color, motion, and copy; the actual feedback stays readable and credible.
3. One action at a time - hero, input, submit, results. No competing CTAs.
4. Dark surface as a spotlight, not gloom - use it to make the accent and results pop.
5. Reveals feel considered, not robotic - loading and result states get one well-timed moment of motion, not scattered micro-animations.
